Project Overview

Study selected revelations in context

This project organizes selected early revelations in the Doctrine and Covenants into a structured dataset and visualizes them through an interactive Tableau dashboard. The goal is to show how revelation came in specific historical moments, locations, and Church circumstances.

Factual

The dataset identifies key people, places, dates, events, and themes connected to early revelations.

Conceptual

The project uses historical and Church history sources to compare context, reliability, and meaning.

Application

The project connects early revelations about priesthood, stewardship, Zion, and Church organization to the modern Church.

Spiritual

Studying the revelations in their historical settings helped me recognize how guidance came during moments of uncertainty, opposition, growth, and responsibility in the early Church.

Historical Context

Why these revelations matter

Unit 5: History and Purpose of the Doctrine and Covenants

The Doctrine and Covenants is a collection of revelations received in real historical circumstances. Mapping the sections by time and place helps show that these revelations responded to specific needs in the Restoration.

Unit 9: Kirtland Opposition and Missouri Expulsion

Many revelations came during periods of opposition, migration, economic pressure, and attempts to build Zion. Seeing the revelations geographically helps show the connection between doctrine, hardship, and Church development.

Patterns Visible in the Dashboard

The geographic distribution shows that the setting of revelation changed as the early Church moved and expanded. Earlier records are concentrated in New York and Pennsylvania, while later revelations increasingly appear in Ohio and Missouri. This movement corresponds with translation work, Church organization, missionary activity, gathering, opposition, and the effort to establish Zion.

The thematic categories also show that revelation addressed changing institutional needs. Individual callings, repentance, and translation are prominent in earlier settings, while priesthood leadership, stewardship, Church government, gathering, and worship became increasingly important as the Church developed.

Research and Build Method

How the project was assembled

  1. Selected major Doctrine and Covenants revelations connected to early Church history.
  2. Recorded dates, locations, recipients, themes, and short historical summaries.
  3. Built a CSV dataset from historical and Church history sources.
  4. Imported the dataset into Tableau Public.
  5. Created geographic, thematic, and section-detail visualizations in Tableau.
  6. Embedded the published dashboard into this public website.

Sources Consulted

Primary and secondary research base

  • The Joseph Smith Papers Project - Used to verify dates, locations, recipients, manuscript history, and historical context.
  • Revelations in Context: The Stories Behind the Revelations of the Doctrine and Covenants
  • BYU Religious Studies Center Digital Repository
  • Doctrine and Covenants section headings and historical notes

Reflection

What the archive shows

Building this project helped me see the Doctrine and Covenants as more than a list of revelations. The dashboard shows that revelation came in moments of decision, pressure, growth, and uncertainty. Studying the revelations by date and location helped me better understand how the Lord guided Joseph Smith and the early Saints step by step.
